## THE ATTRACTIVE CHARACTER — Core Principles (MAV M2 Aula 4)

> "Para a escada de valor funcionar — os funis, a jornada, os anúncios — você precisa entender o conceito de personagem atraente." — Ícaro

### What Is the Attractive Character?

The Attractive Character is the bridge between you and your audience's trust. It's **taking your story and making it wonderful** — not by inventing, but by narrating with structure.

> "Personagem atraente não é ter uma vida épica. É contar sua vida com estrutura narrativa."

### "I Don't Have an Amazing Story" — Breaking the Objection

Everyone thinks their story isn't good enough. The truth:

> **"Ninguém tem. O que muda é que algumas pessoas ficam muito boas em contar história de forma agradável, gerar curiosidade e identificação. Isso é método. É técnica. É aprendível."**

**How to find YOUR story:** Look for turning points in your life:
- A point where everything changed
- People who created obstacles for you
- A great battle you won
- A major reversal or comeback
- A discovery that shifted your perspective

You don't need to invent anything. You need to **identify the elements** and transform them into narrative.

### The "Superpower": Making Your Life Seem Incredible

The ability to narrate your own life in an interesting way IS the Attractive Character. It's not about having an epic life — it's about **structure**.

### Essential Rule: Story of PROGRESS

Every Attractive Character needs a narrative of progress:
- Someone who stays the same for 25 years (same house, car, job, salary) = NOT attractive
- **Permanence is not attractive. Movement is.**
- Progress doesn't need to be a straight line — falls, setbacks, and recoveries make it MORE interesting and human

### The Superman Problem: "Only Winning" Is Boring

> "Ninguém gosta do super-homem porque é perfeito demais."

A story of constant victory bores the audience. What makes it attractive:
- Difficulties and losses
- Recovery and rebuilding
- Learning from failure
- **The fall makes the rise meaningful**

### How to Communicate: Stories and Parables, Never Cold Text

- Learn to tell things through parables
- Tell stories, have conversations
- Avoid "cold text" (bullet points, facts without emotion)
- The Attractive Character lives in the WAY you communicate (continuous storytelling), not just in an "about me" page

## PART 1: Elements of the Attractive Character

### 1. Three backstory/progress stories that can inspire or teach:

**Story 1: The Wake-Up Call (Origin Story)**

David was a Personal Trainer. Had clients, results, confidence. Then life happened. Lost his job. Went through jobs he hated — warehouses, minimum wage, toxic environments. He let himself go. Stopped training. Stopped caring for himself.

And the woman he loved... left him. For another man.

He looked in the mirror and didn't recognize himself. That was the moment he realized: **when a man stops taking care of himself, he loses EVERYTHING else.**

> **Content angle:** *"I didn't lose my body first. I lost my purpose. Then my discipline. Then my energy. Then my confidence. Then her. The body was just the scoreboard — and I was losing at everything."*

**Story 2: The First Client Win (Richard)**

Richard, 43, accountant. Desk job, 9+ hours sitting. Signed up at a gym 2 years ago — NEVER went. Paying monthly, too intimidated to show up. Post-COVID burnout, zero movement, 92kg.

His wife found Coach Teo's content and said: *"Just try it. 25 minutes. What do you have to lose?"*

Richard's first thought: *"25 minutes? That can't work."*

6 months later: 80kg (lost 12kg). Squatting 40kg (from zero). Back pain GONE. Playing football with his son every weekend. His wife said: *"I have my husband back."*

Richard's son said: **"Dad, you're fast now!"**

> **Content angle:** *"Richard was a ghost member at his gym for 2 years. Paying every month, never showing up. Sound familiar? He didn't need a better gym. He needed a better system."*

**Story 3: The System Discovery (The Triple Discovery)**

David didn't discover 25 minutes once. He discovered it THREE times.

1. **Personal necessity:** When he rebuilt himself after hitting rock bottom, he had no gym, no money, no time. He trained with what he had, for as long as he could. That was about 25 minutes. And it WORKED.

2. **Client observation:** As a PT, he noticed a pattern. The clients who trained 60-90 minutes burned out and quit within months. The clients who did shorter, consistent sessions stayed for YEARS. Consistency always beat duration.

3. **Science confirmation:** He found the research backing what he'd already seen: studies showing diminishing returns after 20-30 minutes of resistance training, and that frequency matters more than duration for body composition and health.

> **Content angle:** *"I didn't discover 25 minutes once. I discovered it THREE times. First, my broken life forced me to find the minimum. Then, my clients proved consistency beats duration. Finally, science confirmed what I already knew. Less time. More results. Zero excuses."*

### 2. Two parables (short stories with a lesson):

**Parable 1: The Overflowing Cup**

A CEO came to David wanting the "perfect 6-week program." He had spreadsheets. Research. Questions about optimal rep ranges.

David said: *"Do one pushup tomorrow morning. Just one. Then call me."*

The CEO laughed. *"That's ridiculous. One pushup won't change anything."*

David replied: *"You've had gym memberships for 15 years. How many have you actually used? The cup is already full of plans. We need to empty it first."*

The CEO did one pushup. Then two. Then five. Three months later, he was doing 50 pushups every morning.

> **Lesson:** *"Empty the cup. Start stupidly small. The perfect plan you never do loses to the imperfect plan you do daily."*

**Parable 2: The System That Profits From Your Failure**

David asks a new client: *"How many gym memberships have you paid for and never used?"*

The client laughs nervously. *"Three. Maybe four."*

David explains: That's not your fault. Gyms NEED you to fail. If every member actually showed up, they'd be at 10x capacity. The fitness industry sells hope, not results. Personal trainers charge $150/hour for what you could do in your living room. Supplement companies sell $60 bottles of glorified vitamins. The entire system is designed to keep you paying and failing.

> **Lesson:** *"The system profits from your failure. We profit from your presence. That's the difference. You don't need a gym. You need 25 minutes and a system designed for YOUR life — not for a 22-year-old who trains for a living."*
