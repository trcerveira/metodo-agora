## ORIGIN STORY (REAL)

> "I'm a certified Personal Trainer. I worked in 3 locations. Always running from one place to the next. Training 1 hour + shower + getting dressed = 90 minutes. I did this 3-4 times a week."
>
> "I had training plans. I had coaches. I always quit. And I was left with zero energy for the rest of the day."
>
> "Until I had a week without training. And instead of blaming myself, I asked: what if the problem isn't me? What if the problem is the system?"
>
> "I started training 25 minutes. Better results than with 60. Energy for the whole day. Consistency I never had. Because 25 minutes has no excuse. It can be done at home with a pair of dumbbells."
>
> "If a Personal Trainer couldn't stay consistent with 1-hour workouts — how is a man with an office job, kids, and meetings going to manage?"

### Why this story works:

1. **A PT who failed at his own system** — ultimate credibility. If the expert couldn't do it, the system is broken, not the person.
2. **The discovery came from personal necessity** — not theory, not a book, not a course. Real life forced the solution.
3. **Relatable to EVERY member** — every man in The 25 Club has tried and quit. Coach Teo tried and quit too. The difference: he found the fix.
4. **Proves the method** — he didn't just create 25 Method as a product. He LIVES it. Every day. Because it's the only thing that worked for HIM.

## CHARACTER FLAW (REAL)

> "There are days I disappear. I won't lie. But I'll try every day to show up, say hi, and post my workout."

### Why this flaw is powerful:

- Shows that even the coach has bad days (human, not guru)
- The commitment isn't perfection — it's PRESENCE
- Members think: "If he can fail and come back, so can I"
- Creates permission to be imperfect (reduces shame, increases retention)
- The flaw MATCHES the solution: "25 minutes. Even on bad days. Even when you don't feel like it."

### How to use the flaw inside Skool:

| Situation | What Coach Teo does |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Good day | Posts "25 DONE" + engages with members + coaches |
| Normal day | Posts "25 DONE" + brief check-in |
| Bad day | Posts "oi" or nothing — and that's OK |
| After bad day(s) | Returns without apology, just shows up: "Back. 25 DONE." |

> Rule: NEVER undo progress on bad days. Zero is acceptable. Don't delete, don't cancel, don't quit.

This is the Hyperliquid model:
