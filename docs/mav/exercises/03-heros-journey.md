## PART 1: Identifying Story Elements

### 1. Theme:
**Transformation** — From an invisible, defeated desk worker to a confident, energized man who takes control of his body and his life.

### 2. Protagonist:
**The Client as Hero:** Richard — a real client, not a composite.
- 43 years old, Accountant (desk job, 9+ hours sitting)
- Works from home since COVID
- 92kg (carrying 15kg extra), zero muscle mass
- Chronic lower back pain from sitting all day
- Ghost member at gym for 2 years — paying, never going
- Post-COVID burnout, couldn't play with kids for 5 minutes
- Felt invisible, embarrassed, defeated
- **His desire:** To stop being invisible. To be present for his family.

**25 Method (the brand) as the Mentor/Guide** — Not the hero. Built by a man who fell, rebuilt himself, and created a system to show others the path. The founder is invisible — 100% focused on members inside the community.

### 3. Story elements:
- **Main character:** Richard (the client)
- **Secondary characters:** His wife (who found 25 Method content and pushed him to try), his son (who becomes his motivation), his colleagues (who notice the change on Zoom), the 25 Method system (the mentor)
- **Setting:** Modern remote work life — home office, Zoom meetings, school runs, weekend family time
- **Extraordinary event:** His wife finds 25 Method content on Instagram and says: *"Just try it. 25 minutes. What do you have to lose?"*

## PART 2: The 12 Stages (Based on Richard's Real Journey)

### DEPARTURE

#### 1. The Ordinary World
Richard sits at his home office desk 9+ hours a day. He hasn't exercised since before COVID. He signed up for a gym membership 2 years ago — never went. Too intimidated, too embarrassed, too busy. He pays £40/month for a membership he's never used. His back hurts every morning. He can barely play with his kids for 5 minutes without getting winded. He knows something is wrong but tells himself: *"This is just what happens after 40."*

> **Content angle:** *"If you're paying for a gym you haven't visited in months — you're not lazy. You're invisible. The gym doesn't know your name, doesn't track your progress, doesn't care if you show up. You're a number on a spreadsheet. And that's exactly why you quit."*

#### 2. The Call to Adventure
Richard's wife is scrolling through Instagram. She sees a Reel: *"You're not getting old. You're getting still."* She sends it to Richard. He watches it. Then watches another. Then another. Something clicks — this guy isn't talking about six-packs and gym selfies. He's talking about RICHARD.

That night, his wife says: *"Just try it. 25 minutes. What do you have to lose?"*

Richard's first thought: *"25 minutes? That can't work. I need at least an hour."*

But he's desperate. He clicks the link.

> **Content angle:** *"Every transformation starts with someone who cares enough to push you. For Richard, it was his wife. For you, it might be this video. 25 minutes. What do you have to lose?"*

#### 3. Refusal of the Call
Richard has tried before. Gym membership — never went. Downloaded a fitness app — too many exercises he didn't understand. Bought resistance bands during COVID — they're in a drawer.

He tells himself: *"I don't have time." "25 minutes can't be enough." "I'm too out of shape to start." "Maybe next month, after the project deadline."*

Every excuse is a wall built from past failures.

> **Content angle:** *"You've tried before. I know. And you're carrying the weight of those failures too — not just the physical weight. Here's what nobody told you: it wasn't your fault. You weren't lazy. You were invisible. The programs weren't designed for your life."*

#### 4. Meeting the Mentor
Richard clicks the link in bio. Lands on the 25 Method page. The copy speaks directly to his life — sitting 9 hours, back pain, ghost gym membership. He signs up for The 25 Club free trial (7 days free, card required) on Skool.

The onboarding (The Ignition) doesn't talk about reps and sets. It talks about Richard's LIFE. About sitting 9 hours a day. About the back pain. About feeling like a ghost at the gym. Richard thinks: *"How does this system know exactly what I'm going through?"*

He starts Day 1. The first lesson says: *"The best workout is the one you actually do. Tomorrow morning, before your first meeting, give it 25 minutes. That's all."*

> **Content angle:** The Instagram content, landing page, and Skool onboarding ARE this step. Every piece of content is an invitation to "meet the system."

#### 5. Crossing the First Threshold
Day 1 of The Ignition. Richard wakes up 30 minutes early. Does his first 25-minute workout in his living room. It's brutal — harder than expected. He can barely do 5 bodyweight squats. His hip flexors are screaming. He can't hold a plank for 15 seconds.

But he finishes. And as he sits at his desk, something is different. His back doesn't hurt. He has energy at his 9am meeting. He feels... something he hasn't felt in years.

> **Content angle:** *"Day 1 isn't about results. It's about PROOF. Proof to yourself that 25 minutes exists in your day. That your body can still respond. Day 1 is the most important day — not because of what it does to your body, but because of what it does to your belief."*

---

### INITIATION

#### 6. Tests, Allies, and Enemies
**Tests:**
- Week 2: Richard's project deadline hits. He works until midnight. The old Richard would skip. The new Richard sets his alarm anyway. 25 minutes. Done.
- Week 3: A colleague mocks him on a Zoom call: *"What, you're doing home workouts now? Like a YouTube fitness mum?"*

**Allies:**
- His wife notices he has more energy in the evenings. She starts joining for the morning mobility routine
- Other men in The 25 Club community are going through the same thing. They message each other: *"Day 12. Back didn't hurt today."*
- The community check-in on Day 10: *"You're still here. Most people aren't. That already makes you different."*

**Enemies:**
- His own internal voice: *"This is temporary. You'll quit like always."*
- The comfort of the desk — it's easier to sit than to move
- The gym industry ads: *"JOIN NOW — 50% OFF"* — trying to pull him back to the system that failed him

> **Content angle:** *"Week 2 is where most people quit. The novelty is gone. The results aren't visible yet. This is where the SYSTEM matters more than motivation. You don't need to feel like it. You need a REASON. Find your reason."*

#### 7. Approach (Preparation)
Month 2. Richard has completed the 7-day challenge and enrolled in The 25-Minute Protocol. He now trains automatically — it's as routine as brushing his teeth. He's learning about posture, about why desk workers need mobility more than muscle, about how his hip flexors affect his back.

He starts making better food choices — not because of a diet, but because he has more energy and makes clearer decisions. The compound effect begins.

His wife says: *"You seem different. More energy."*

> **Content angle:** *"Nobody talks about the compound effect of 25 minutes. After 30 days, you've trained for 12.5 hours. After 60 days, 25 hours. That's more than most gym members get in 6 months — because they go for 90 minutes, burn out, and quit."*

#### 8. The Ordeal (Central Crisis)
Month 3. Progress stalls. Richard hasn't lost weight in 2 weeks. His back pain came back after a stressful all-hands meeting and a week of 12-hour days. He messages the community: *"Maybe this doesn't work long-term."*

The founder responds inside the community: *"This is the dip. Every single person I've coached hits it between week 8 and 12. It's not failure — it's your body adapting. Here's what we adjust."*

The program is modified. Progressive overload added. Richard pushes through.

> **Content angle:** *"If you hit a plateau at month 3, congratulations. You lasted 10x longer than most people. Now the real work begins — and this is where most programs lose you, because they don't have a plan for the dip. We do."*

#### 9. The Reward
Month 5-6. Richard steps on the scale: 80kg. He's lost 12kg. He's squatting 40kg. But the number isn't what matters. What matters:
- His back pain is **GONE**
- He played football with his son for 45 minutes last Saturday — and wasn't destroyed after
- His wife told him: *"I have my husband back"*
- Colleagues on Zoom asked: *"What have you been doing? You look younger."*
- He looks at old photos and doesn't feel shame — he feels pride

And his son said the words that changed everything: **"Dad, you're fast now!"**

> **Content angle:** *"The scale is the last thing that changes. The FIRST things that change: energy, posture, confidence, sleep, mental clarity. By the time the scale moves, you've already been reborn."*

---

### RETURN

#### 10. The Road Back
Richard's identity has shifted. He's no longer "the guy who should work out." He IS someone who trains daily. It's who he is. He starts sharing his journey with colleagues. Two of them ask for the 25 Method link. His wife does the mobility routine with him every morning.

He realizes: this wasn't just about losing weight. It was about proving to himself that he could COMMIT to something outside of work.

> **Content angle:** *"The moment you stop 'trying to get fit' and start being someone who trains — everything changes. It's not willpower anymore. It's identity. First you build the habit. Then the habit builds you."*

#### 11. Resurrection
Month 8. A work conference. 4 days, 2 cities, back-to-back meetings, hotel rooms, restaurants. The OLD Richard would have used this as an excuse. The NEW Richard packs his resistance band, does 25 minutes every morning in his hotel room, and comes back home feeling better than when he left.

His boss comments: *"You handled that trip like a machine."* Richard smiles. He knows why.

> **Content angle:** *"The true test isn't when conditions are perfect. It's when conditions are terrible and you STILL show up. That's the 25-minute advantage — it's so short, there's literally no excuse that works."*

#### 12. Return with the Elixir
Month 12. Richard is a different man. Not just physically (80kg, strong posture, visible muscle tone) — but mentally and emotionally. He's more patient with his kids. More present with his wife. More focused at work. He sleeps 7 hours. His annual check-up came back clean for the first time in years.

He writes in the community: *"This system didn't just fix my body. It fixed my relationship with myself."*

Richard climbs through The 25 Club community levels — from Recruit to Veteran to Centurion (unlocking the Affiliate Playbook). He helps new Recruits through their Week 2. The cycle continues. He becomes a walking testimonial and earns 50% recurring commission on every referral.

> **Content angle:** *"This isn't a 12-week program. It's a LIFE program that starts with 12 weeks. Richard didn't just lose weight — he became visible again. To his wife. To his kids. To himself. That's the movement."*
